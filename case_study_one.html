Existing Problem (Overview) :

The existing app had a bad user experience with no design guidelines, and UI elements, which made it difficult to the user to find or buy the products through app, even the app is serviceable in multiple states of India, multiple language option was not available for the user. (Detailed overview is discussed in the phase below)

What can be Done ?

Design a mobile app that facilitates farmers to purchase agricultural products easily. It aims to simplify the process of viewing products, checking prices, and making purchases online across 7+ states with multiple languages. The app will feature a user-friendly interface, intuitive navigation, and vibrant UI color schemes to enhance the user experience. (Detailed overview is discussed in the phase below)

Empathize

The goal of this phase is to understand customers, by searching and gathering information about customer needs. During this phase, used several different tools, such as interviews, observations, and surveys.

User Engagement: Conducted comprehensive user engagement analysis to identify active users and understand their behaviours and preferences.

Geographic Insights: Explored the geographical distribution of our user base to pinpoint regions with the highest user concentration and growth potential.

Demographic Profiling: Collected detailed user personas by collecting data on gender, age groups, and other relevant demographics to tailor our product to their needs effectively.

Interest Exploration: Investigated user interests in agri-related products and the feedback on buying them.

Define

In this phase, we categorize the collected data from the user research, created user personas and their journey. To understand the user pain points and their requirements, we determined and identified :

  • Our customers
  • Their problems
  • The solution to their problem

User Personas

Based on our insights and user engagement from the research methods, we created two personas that captured the essence of our users and their characteristics. We also formed empathy maps to understand their needs and challenges.

Task Analysis & Journey Maps

The personas and empathy maps helped us outline challenges faced, which we then used to create a journey map highlighting the pain points and the opportunities for improvement.

Ideate

The problem

The personas, empathy, and Journey map help us understand the problems and I have listed some of the problems from the outcome which are outlined below

Complexity in Purchasing: Farmers often find it challenging to buy agricultural products online due to complicated interfaces and language barriers.

Inefficient User Experience: The existing app offers a dull user experience with a cluttered layout, making it difficult for farmers to find and purchase the products they need.

Language Barriers: Many farmers may not be proficient in English or other commonly used languages, leading to confusion and frustration when navigating the app.

Poor UI Color Schemes: The current UI color schemes may not be visually appealing or may not convey the app's purpose effectively.

The Goal

Streamlined User Experience: The primary goal is to provide farmers with a seamless and intuitive user experience. They should be able to navigate the app effortlessly, from product search to purchase confirmation.

Multilingual Support: The app should support multiple languages, allowing farmers to use it comfortably in their preferred language. This includes both product information and interface elements.

One-Click Purchase:The app should enable farmers to make purchases with minimal effort. Ideally, they should be able to add products to their cart and complete the transaction with just one click.

Appealing UI Color Schemes: Improving the UI color schemes to align with the agricultural theme and evoke a sense of trust and ease for farmers is essential. Consider using earthy tones, greens, and other colors associated with farming to create a visually pleasing environment.

Test and Evaluate

  • High User Satisfaction: Users were satisfied with the app's functionality, features, and the overall shopping experience
  • Positive User Experience: The 84% usability score indicates that users had a positive overall experience when interacting with the prototype.
  • Simplified Shopping Process: Users have appreciated the streamlined shopping process, from product selection to checkout, making it easier to make purchases.
  • Responsive Design: A high usability score indicate that the app prototype performed well on various devices and screen sizes, providing a consistent experience.
  • High Usability Approval: An 84% positive usability response indicates that users found the e-commerce mobile app prototype is highly usable and user-friendly.
